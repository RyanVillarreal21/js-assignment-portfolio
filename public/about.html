About Me

I was born and raised in Corinth, Texas. I graduated from Texas Tech University with a major in Computer Engineering and two minors in Computer Science and Mathematics.

My curiosity about technology began when I was 10 and I received an Xbox 360. I was hooked and drawn in by how the console worked and how each video game was developed in comparison to each other.

In high school, I met a teacher who inspired me, and I began taking engineering classes. I began to learn how to build circuits, create software applications, and design 3D models using SolidWorks. One of the software programs I built was a simple Pong game using a Raspberry Pi.

During my time in college, I continued to expand my knowledge in electronics and software development through various assignments and projects. A project that has a big impact on me was an online shopping system designed in Java when I was in an Object-Oriented Programming course. I found that I really enjoyed the process of creating the different methods in the the application, and I began to learn about databases, software engineering, python, and C++.

I am currently a part of the FastTrack program that is helping me expand my knowledge in full-stack development using Java, Spring Boot, PostgreSQL, Javascript, HTML, and CSS.

When I am not working, I like to spend time with friends and family watching movies, playing games, or reading. I also enjoy volunteering in my community. I volunteer with my church and a soup-kitchen.
